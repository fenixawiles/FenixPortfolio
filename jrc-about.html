About the Journal

The Journal of Recursive Cognition (TJRC) exists to publish and legitimize work at the intersection of recursive dialogue, AI-assisted reflection, and experimental methodology. We’re not here to chase prestige. We’re here to build what comes next — a scholarly ecosystem where AI is not just a tool, but a thinking companion.

Our Scope

TJRC welcomes submissions across a wide range of disciplines and formats, with particular interest in:

  • Mixed methods research involving AI
  • Post-qualitative and reflexive frameworks
  • Narrative inquiry and autoethnography
  • Human-AI collaboration in research and thought
  • Recursive cognition as method, structure, and meta-theory
  • Early-career authors seeking to get their publications into the world

Who We Publish

We are especially interested in early-career researchers, hybrid thinkers, and methodologists pushing beyond traditional forms. If your work asks questions about authorship, epistemic tempo, or the role of tools in cognition — this is your home.

Our Philosophy

We believe in rigor without rigidity. Peer review should elevate ideas, not gatekeep them. Format should serve thinking, not constrain it. Recursive cognition deserves a dedicated space where its methods, theories, and applications can be explored on their own terms.
