Vol. 1, Issue 1 — Coming Soon

The inaugural issue of The Journal of Recursive Cognition is in development. It will explore the foundations of recursive inquiry, including the use of AI in reflexive, process-based scholarship.


Recursive Cognition in Practice: How AI Dialogue Generated and Analyzed Its Own Methodology

Fenix Wiles

Currently in final peer review at the International Journal of Qualitative Methods (IJQM), this article introduces Recursive Cognition in Practice (RCIP) as a structured, AI-mediated dialogue method. It demonstrates how AI can serve simultaneously as co-writer, co-theorist, and co-analyst — recursively producing both knowledge and its own methodology in real time.

Although this article provides the theoretical and methodological foundation for the journal’s creation, the author — as founder and Editor-in-Chief — will not publish it within The Journal of Recursive Cognition in order to preserve editorial integrity and avoid conflicts of interest. Upon publication, a link to the final article and the founder’s ORCID/DOI will be made publicly accessible through the journal’s site.
